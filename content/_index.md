Hi, I'm Lu Shen.

I am a postdoctoral researcher at Texas A&M University, where I study spatially resolved star formation in galaxies at intermediate redshifts using *JWST* and *HST* data from surveys including CEERS, NGDEEP, CANDELS, and UVCANDELS.

Before joining TAMU, I was at the University of Science and Technology of China (USTC), working with Guilin Liu on powerful outflows driven by **red quasars** at $z\sim0.5$.

I obtained my Ph.D. at the University of California, Davis, supervised by Lori Lubin. My research has focused on **the role of environments** (i.e., clusters and protoclusters) in galaxy evolution, using the **ORELSE** survey for clusters at $z\sim1$ and the **C3VO** survey for protoclusters at $z\sim2-5$. <!--Particularly, I studied environmental effects on radio galaxies, where radio emission is due to AGN and/or star formation activity, allowing us to study the coevolution of AGN and star formation. -->
