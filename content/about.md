I was born and raised in Beijing, China, and became interested in astronomy while studying at UCSB as an undergraduate exchange student. I began my astronomy journey by studying environmental effects on galaxy evolution. By environment, I mean large-scale structures such as clusters and protoclusters, as well as local density. I also became interested in feedback from radio galaxies, their surrounding environments, and how interactions with the interstellar medium shape radio-jet morphology. Now, I focus more on normal galaxies without AGN and how they grow their stellar mass.
